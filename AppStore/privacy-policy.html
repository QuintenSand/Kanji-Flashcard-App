Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 12, 2026

Kanji Flashcard App ("the App") is developed by Quinten Sandberg. This privacy policy explains how the App handles your information.

Data Collection

The App does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. All study progress, preferences, and statistics are stored locally on your device using iOS's built-in storage (UserDefaults). No information ever leaves your device.

No Accounts or Sign-Up

The App does not require or offer user accounts, email addresses, or any form of registration. You can use the App immediately after downloading it.

No Analytics or Tracking

The App does not include any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking frameworks. There are no cookies, no device fingerprinting, and no usage telemetry.

No Network Requests

The App works entirely offline. It does not make any network requests, connect to any servers, or require an internet connection to function. The only network-related feature is local push notifications for study reminders, which are scheduled on-device through iOS and do not involve any external server.

Third-Party Services

The App does not integrate with any third-party services, SDKs, or APIs.

Data Storage and Deletion

Your study data is stored only on your device. You can reset all progress at any time from the Settings screen within the App. Uninstalling the App removes all associated data from your device.

Children's Privacy

The App does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. It is safe for users of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the revised version will be posted on this page with an updated date. Since the App collects no data, material changes are unlikely.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can reach out at quinten.sandberg@hotmail.com or open an issue on the GitHub repository.
